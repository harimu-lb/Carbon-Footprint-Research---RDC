Why Act Now

Three pressures
are converging — simultaneously.

Japan's regulatory trajectory

The amended GX Promotion Act (2025) introduced mandatory participation in Japan's emissions trading system. Event industry exposure to Scope 3 disclosure requirements is growing. Early measurement creates a compliance head start worth more every year you delay.

Sponsor selection criteria

In 2025–2026, global festival sponsors are requesting environmental impact data as a condition of partnership renewal. CSR departments evaluate waste diversion, energy source, and emissions per attendee. Festivals with data gain leverage; those without are deprioritised.

The first-mover window

Right now.

No peer Japanese festival has published a verified carbon baseline. The window to be first — and to use that position with sponsors, press, and partners — is open. It will not stay open indefinitely.

European festivals such as Glastonbury and Primavera Sound have used their sustainability credentials to secure multi-year brand partnerships. The playbook is proven.

The Research

Two phases. One
rigorous output.

Phase 1

Carbon Footprint Assessment

A full baseline across all major emission categories — energy, transport, suppliers, waste — using the GHG Protocol and IPCC emission factors. The same methodology used by Glastonbury and Download Festival.

The output includes 3–5 ranked reduction scenarios with modelled CO₂e savings, cost estimates, and ROI ranges for each.

Phase 2

Attendee Sustainability Survey

A short, RDC-approved survey — deployed through the mailing list — measuring attendee support for sustainability initiatives, willingness to pay, and brand perception.

Target: 362 completed responses. All data anonymous. Survey instrument submitted for RDC review before any deployment.

Academic rigour: This research follows a structured empirical design supervised by faculty at Waseda University's Graduate School of Business and Finance. It is subject to academic peer review and held to the standards of published sustainability consultancy work.

What RDC Receives

Five deliverables.
Zero cost.

  • Full Carbon Report (Confidential) — Complete footprint baseline with category-level breakdown, methodology notes, and data sources. For internal use only.
  • Reduction Scenario Analysis — 3–5 ranked interventions with modelled CO₂e reduction, cost impact, ROI range, and implementation complexity.
  • Sponsor-Ready Environmental Summary — A concise 1–2 page document formatted for sponsorship decks and press releases. RDC retains full editorial approval before use.
  • Replicable Methodology Template — A structured data collection and calculation framework RDC can apply independently in future years.
  • Full Confidentiality — RDC reviews all public-facing content before submission. Anonymisation of RDC's name available on request. No financial data published in unaggregated form.

The Data Ask

Specific. Bounded.
Already exists.

The data request covers records generated in the normal course of festival operations. RDC does not need to prepare anything from scratch. Where records are unavailable, published benchmarks are substituted.
