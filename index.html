The Context

A timely opportunity
for Japanese festivals
to lead on sustainability data.

RDC has built one of Asia's most respected underground festival brands — defined by curatorial integrity and a community-oriented ethos. As sustainability expectations grow globally, understanding a festival's environmental impact is becoming increasingly relevant for organizers, sponsors, and audiences alike.

This proposal offers RDC the tools to understand and act on that impact, at no cost.

Why Now

Three trends worth
understanding — together.

Japan's evolving regulatory landscape

Japan's GX Promotion Act and the 2050 Carbon Neutral commitment are driving sustainability measurement across the corporate supply chain. Understanding an event's carbon footprint positions organizers ahead of a direction of travel that is already underway.

Shifting sponsor expectations

In 2025–2026, corporate sponsors — particularly those with their own ESG reporting obligations — are showing greater interest in the environmental practices of their event partners. Having data available opens conversations that would otherwise be difficult to have.

An open space in Japan

Right now.

No major Japanese music festival has yet published a verified carbon footprint. There is a genuine opportunity for a festival like RDC — with its community-oriented values — to be among the first to understand and communicate its impact.

Festivals such as Glastonbury and Primavera Sound have found that sustainability data opens new conversations with sponsors, press, and partners. The experience is instructive, even if every festival's context is different.

The Research

Two phases. One
grounded output.

Phase 1

Carbon Footprint Assessment

A baseline across the main emission categories — energy, transport, suppliers, waste — using the GHG Protocol and IPCC emission factors. The same methodology applied by festivals such as Glastonbury and Download.

The analysis would explore 3–5 potential reduction scenarios, modelling their estimated CO₂e impact and indicative cost implications.

Phase 2

Attendee Sustainability Survey

A short, RDC-approved survey — distributed through the mailing list — exploring attendee perspectives on sustainability, environmental values, and the festival experience.

Target: approximately 357 completed responses. All data fully anonymous. The survey instrument would be shared with RDC for review before any deployment.

About the researcher: Halim Saade is an IMBA student at the Waseda Business School with professional experience as an Environmental Project Manager and Event/Festival Organiser. His research focuses on the intersection of sustainability and financial performance in the Japanese entertainment event industry. This research is conducted to academic standards and is subject to faculty supervision and peer review.

Potential Outputs for RDC

Practical findings.
No cost to participate.

Subject to the data available and the findings of the research, RDC could receive the following:

  • Carbon Footprint Summary (Confidential) — A breakdown of estimated emissions by category, with methodology and data sources documented. Intended for RDC's internal use.
  • Reduction Scenario Overview — An exploration of 3–5 potential interventions, with indicative CO₂e impact and cost considerations for each.
  • Communications-Ready Summary — If RDC wishes, a concise summary of findings suitable for sharing with sponsors or partners. RDC would retain full editorial approval over any such document.
  • Data Collection Framework — A record of the methodology and data inputs used, which RDC could reference if it wished to repeat or build on the assessment in future years.
  • Full Confidentiality — RDC reviews all content before any academic submission. No operational or financial data will be published in identifiable form. Anonymisation of RDC's name in the published thesis is available on request.

What the Research Would Need

Specific. Bounded.
Already exists.

The data request is limited to records generated in the normal course of running the festival — nothing that would need to be created from scratch. Where records are unavailable or incomplete, published industry benchmarks would be used instead.

Category What Would Be Useful
EnergyGenerator fuel receipts (litres of diesel by unit); electricity invoices (kWh); power equipment rental agreements
WasteWaste contractor reports (kg by stream: general, recycling, food/organic)
Operations TransportStaff and artist travel records by origin and mode; vendor delivery manifests
SuppliersFood and beverage vendor list with approximate origin location
AttendeesTicket purchaser postcode or prefecture distribution — anonymised or aggregated is sufficient

All data would be handled under a formal confidentiality agreement, signed before any transfer takes place. Raw operational data would not be shared with any third party without the written approval of the RDC management.
